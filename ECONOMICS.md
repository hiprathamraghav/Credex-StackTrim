# Economics

Assume a converted Credex customer buys $25,000/year of discounted AI credits and Credex earns a 12% gross margin. That makes one converted customer worth about $3,000 in gross profit before support and sales time. If larger startups buy $100,000/year, the value can be much higher, but the MVP should not depend on enterprise whales.

Channel CAC can stay low if distribution is founder-led. A Show HN or X launch may cost only time, so the cash CAC is near $0 but the labor cost could be 6-10 hours. Founder Slack/community outreach may take 20 personalized messages to get 5 audits; at an assumed $30/hour labor cost and 2 hours of work, that is $60 for 5 audits or $12 per audit. Direct DMs may be higher effort: 50 messages, 10 audits, 2 high-savings leads, or roughly $9-$15 per audit depending on labor assumptions.

A simple funnel: 1,000 visitors -> 300 audit starts -> 150 completed audits -> 45 email captures -> 10 consultations -> 2 credit purchases. At $3,000 gross profit each, that is $6,000 gross profit from 1,000 targeted visitors. The tool becomes profitable if audit completion stays above 15%, consultation booking from completed audits stays above 3%, and purchase conversion from consultation stays above 15%.

To drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex would need roughly $8.3M/year in credit volume at a 12% take rate. If the average customer buys $25k/year, that is about 333 active customers. If the average is $75k/year, it is 111 customers. The tool would need repeatable distribution and enough high-savings audits to feed sales weekly.
