# Metrics

The North Star metric is qualified audits completed per week. This fits a B2B lead-generation tool because the product creates value when a real operator completes enough of the flow to reveal a measurable savings opportunity.

The first input metric is visitor-to-audit-start rate, because the landing page must make the calculator feel lightweight enough for a cold visitor from Hacker News or X. The second is audit-start-to-audit-complete rate, because abandonment would signal the form feels too tedious or asks for confusing details. The third is high-savings lead capture rate, because Credex only benefits commercially when users with meaningful savings identify themselves after seeing value.

I would instrument page view, tool selected, audit submitted, audit completed, share link copied, lead captured, and consultation CTA clicked. The first pivot trigger is fewer than 20% of audit starters completing the audit after 100 starts. That would mean the calculator is too heavy, the copy is unclear, or users do not understand what data to enter.
