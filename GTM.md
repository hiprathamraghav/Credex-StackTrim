# GTM

The target user is a technical founder, engineering manager, or ops-minded CTO at a 5-50 person startup that has started paying for multiple AI tools but has not formalized procurement. They are likely seeing Cursor, ChatGPT Team, Claude, Copilot, and API charges scattered across founder cards, engineering budgets, and product experiments.

Right before they want this tool, they are probably searching “Cursor pricing team worth it,” “ChatGPT Team vs Plus,” “Claude Team cost,” or scrolling posts about AI bills getting out of hand. The emotional trigger is not “AI spend management” as a category; it is the moment they realize their AI tooling bill grew from a few individual subscriptions into a real monthly line item.

The first 100 users should come from specific zero-budget channels: Show HN with the anonymized report angle, Indie Hackers build-in-public updates, founder Slack groups, college startup groups, and X replies to posts about Cursor/Claude/OpenAI bills. I would also DM founders who recently posted about AI agents, engineering productivity, or infra costs and offer to run their stack through the calculator.

Credex’s unfair channel is pricing credibility. Most calculators can say “switch tools,” but Credex can say “you may be paying retail for credits that can be sourced at a discount.” That makes the tool feel closer to a procurement insight than a generic SaaS calculator.

Week-1 traction if it works: 300 unique visitors, 90 audit starts, 45 completed audits, 15 email captures, 5 high-savings consultation clicks, and at least 3 conversations with founders who ask whether the credit savings are actually available.
